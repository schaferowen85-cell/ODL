Why a psychotherapist

IQ got you the seat. EQ keeps the revenue.

IQ measures the ability to learn. The application of that learning is EQ. Creativity, Confirmation, and Communication, the three of them, are EQ, and they are what separate a revenue leader who can hold a team together from one who can only hold a spreadsheet.

Here is what missing EQ looks like in an ordinary week. A team runs A/B tests for a month with no hypothesis going in, then cannot explain why the winner won, so they learn nothing they can use again. That is Confirmation missing.

A leadership meeting runs three hours with one person talking ninety-five percent of the time, and the next meeting opens on the exact same problems, untouched. That is Communication missing.

I have watched both happen inside profitable companies full of smart people. The IQ was never the problem. The system that applies it was, and that system is what we build here.

That work is harder when the team is not in the same room, which is most teams now. Putting a registered psychotherapist in the process is not soft. It is the part most leaders are never coached on, and it is where the durable gains come from. This is the work my wife and co-author Elisha and I built into the HELP Operating System, and it is why this program is one of the few that takes it seriously.

How the sessions run

Every session moves through HELP.

This is not a venting session with a smart person. It is a method, the same operating system I install in leadership teams, run one-on-one on whatever you walked in carrying.

  • Hear.  We get the whole situation on the table before we touch it. What is actually happening with the number, the team, and you, including the part you have not said out loud.
  • Evidence.  We separate fact from interpretation. The thing you are sure is true and the thing you are assuming are rarely the same. This is where most bad revenue calls get caught.
  • Learn.  We find the pattern. Most of what feels unprecedented to you, I have seen a version of. We name what is really going on.
  • Proceed.  We end on a decision and a move. Not a framework for later. The next action, owned, with reasoning you can defend to your team or your board.
