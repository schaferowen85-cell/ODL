What I do

I help companies through the transitions they don’t know how to navigate.

How to go after their market. How to use new technology to leapfrog their competitors. How to work as one team again. That’s the work.

The market shifted. Technology moved faster than most organizations could absorb. And a lot of teams quietly broke when they went remote and never learned how to operate that way. The companies that call me know something is wrong. They usually can’t name all three at once.

From the outside it looks like a revenue problem. On the ground it’s three layered ones: how they go after the market, how they use the technology already in front of them, and how the team actually works together. I see the system, not just the symptom.

Two ways I work

One operator. Two services.

The operator

I streamline the org and make the change hold.

I come in and rebuild how a company goes after its market. AI and the latest technology, sales and GTM systems, and live leadership coaching, wired together so the org runs on intention instead of activity.

Then I staff it. I bring in resources from my network and put the right people in the right seats, so the change survives after I step back. The goal is never dependence on me. It’s a team that operates like they share a building, even when they are spread across the world.

The think-tank builder

I build the team that figures out how the company has to think.

Some problems aren’t revenue problems. They’re problems of how an organization has to think to move forward. For those I build think-tank teams from my network and stand them up around the question that actually matters.

This is where the work crosses industries that aren’t strictly revenue plays. Education is the clearest example. It’s strategy and thought leadership, built by a team assembled for the problem in front of us.

The method

It all runs on H.E.L.P.

Hear, Evidence, Learn, Proceed. It’s the four-step practice behind every meeting I run, every coaching session, every hard conversation I prepare a leader for. It’s how I keep a team transparent about what’s actually true before anyone decides what to do.

It didn’t come from a boardroom. During the pandemic I watched CEOs and leaders come apart. Panicking, isolated, carrying things from home into the work with nowhere to put them. That drove me deep into the behavioural sciences, solving for one thing: how to get through almost any conversation, at any moment.

I co-built it with my wife, Elisha, who trained at McMaster and runs her own counselling practice. She brought the clinical foundation. I brought the business conversations. Together we built the system that everything else here stands on. Read more about HELP or meet Elisha.

How the engagement runs

Surgical. In for what matters.

In most companies I do this part-time. I’m there for the critical meetings and the one-on-one coaching, not the calendar. The intelligence is already inside the company. Most teams just don’t know how to use it together. My job is to fix that, then get out of the way.

I coach leaders in their actual seats, in real situations, for muscle memory. How to run the meeting. How to have the conversation with an employee, a client, a board. Multiple leaders at once, in many cases. I learned to build and run teams across continents at NetSuite, in Hong Kong on a call with Sydney while the rest of the org sat in Toronto and San Mateo. We were built to work remote before most people were. That’s the muscle I install.

Where it happens

Mostly in the room.

This is what I’m known for. Sales kickoffs. Leadership off-sites. Sales teams that need to be rebooted fast. Management teams that need to be realigned around one plan. Live, hands-on, in front of the people who have to carry it after I leave. A framework on a slide doesn’t change behaviour. Reps and conversations do.
