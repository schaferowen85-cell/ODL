The H.E.L.P. Operating System

Four disciplines.
Decisions that stick.

Co-developed with Alicia Schafer over ten years. Rooted in behavioral science and motivational interviewing. It converts urgency into discipline. Noise drops. Progress accelerates.

H Hear

Before you solve, listen.

Most leadership breakdowns are diagnosed before the full picture is understood. Hear creates the conditions for people to say what is actually true. You cannot fix what you have not actually seen.

E Evidence

Separate fact from interpretation.

The forecast is a fact. The story about why it missed is an interpretation. Evidence discipline keeps the room anchored to what is real and measurable. It kills the blame cycle before it starts.

L Learn

Fluid learning, not fixed playbooks.

Markets shift faster than most GTM playbooks are updated. Learn builds the habit of continuously updating what you know rather than defending what you already decided. Curiosity replaces certainty.

P Proceed

Move with ownership, not permission.

Decisions made with full context and clear ownership compress time. Proceed turns the first three phases into committed action. The team moves because they understand, not because they were told to.

What H.E.L.P. is not

"Not a workshop. Not a retreat. It is the way decisions get made every week until the team internalizes it."

The outcome

"Urgency drops. Ownership rises. The revenue plan becomes believable again because the team believes it themselves."

The Three Cs

Future-proofing
the GTM machine.

The H.E.L.P. OS gets the company aligned. The Three Cs keep it sharp as markets shift. This is the frame Chris leaves behind with every team he works with.

Creativity
Think differently.

The best GTM responses do not come from the playbook. They come from teams who are safe to think differently and fast enough to act on what they find. Creativity is a discipline, not a personality trait.

Confirmation
Close the handoff gap.

Decisions fail at the handoff. Confirmation is the practice of owning every decision before the meeting ends. Not in a follow-up. In the room, before people leave.

Communication
One version of the truth.

The revenue plan fails when the story about it is inconsistent. Finance hears one version. Sales hears another. The board hears a third. Communication discipline means one version, told the same way, at every level.
