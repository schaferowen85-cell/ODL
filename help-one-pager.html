Hear · Evidence · Learn · Proceed

The four moves

Step 01
Hear

Open with “What is going on?” and hold the silence. Every follow-up starts with what, where, who, when, or how. Never why. Close by reflecting it back in their words: “Did I get that right?”

Step 02
Evidence

Sort into two columns, facts and interpretations. A fact survives “How do we know that?” Words like obviously and clearly mark a story. End with the short list of what is still missing.

Step 03
Learn

Never give the answer. Ask “Where have you seen this before?” and let them pull the pattern from their own experience. The lesson they find sticks. The one you hand them does not.

Step 04
Proceed

Ask “What are you leaning toward?” Then three words: “Trust your gut.” The decision has to feel theirs, not a lightly edited version of yours.

You are off the method when

  • You fill the silence after “What is going on?” Most leaders collapse it inside fifteen seconds. The collapse is the failure.
  • You ask why during Hear. Why asks people to defend. Save it for the evidence, never aim it at the person.
  • You let “obviously” or “clearly” into the fact column. Those words are how a guess dresses up as a fact.
  • You start solving before the sorting is done. The first fact you find is rarely the whole picture.
  • You name the winner in Proceed. Surface the options and hand over the call. Only an owned decision gets conviction.

The same four moves hold whether the conversation is two minutes in the hallway or a six-month turnaround. The depth changes. The moves do not. Run it until the team runs the cadence without you.
